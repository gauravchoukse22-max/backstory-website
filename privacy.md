# Backstory — Privacy Policy

_Effective date: 2026-07-15_

**Backstory is local-first: by default, none of your data leaves your device. The only time
any data reaches a server is if you choose to create an account to use Family Sharing.**

Backstory is provided by **Gaurav Choukse** (an individual developer). Contact:
**gauravchoukse22@gmail.com**.

Everything you put into Backstory — your milestones, dates, stories, photos, and voice
memos — is stored **only on your device**. There are no analytics and no tracking of any
kind. You do **not** need an account, and without one the app is fully functional and
entirely on-device. Your memories never leave your iPhone or Android phone except in ways
you explicitly choose:

- **Backups:** When you tap _Export a backup_, a backup file is created and handed to your
  phone's share sheet so **you** can save or send it wherever you like. We never receive it.
  You can protect the file with a password, in which case it is encrypted on your device
  (authenticated AES‑256‑GCM) before it leaves the app.
- **Device cloud backup:** Because your memories live in the app's storage on your phone, they
  are included in your device's standard **Apple iCloud** or **Google** device backup if you
  have that enabled. That data is held by Apple/Google under your own account, governed by
  their privacy policies — not by us.
- **iCloud backup (opt‑in, in the app):** If you tap _Back up now_ under iCloud backup, the app
  writes an **encrypted** copy of your memories to **your own iCloud** (Apple's iCloud Drive,
  in a private app container). The backup is encrypted on your device with a key stored in your
  device's secure keychain before it is written, so it is unreadable by anyone — including us
  and Apple. We never receive it and have no access to it. You choose when to back up and when
  to restore; nothing is sent automatically.
- **Sharing a memory:** If you use the system share sheet to send a photo or memory, it goes
  wherever you send it, via your phone's standard sharing — we are not involved.

We do **not** sell or rent your personal information, we do **not** show ads, and we do
**not** use your memories to train AI or for any other purpose.

### Optional accounts & Family Sharing

Family Sharing is an **optional** feature that lets you share chosen milestones with family
members. It is **off unless you create an account** — and you never have to. If you do sign
up, here is exactly what happens and what we hold:

- **Account:** You create an account with your **email and a password**, or with **Sign in
  with Apple**. Signing in with Apple lets you use Apple's **Hide My Email** relay, in which
  case we only ever see a private relay address, not your real email.
- **What we store (on our behalf, by our hosting provider Supabase):**
  - your **email address** (or Apple relay address) and an **account identifier**;
  - a **display name** you choose (what family members see);
  - only the **milestones you explicitly choose to share** to a circle — their date, title,
    notes, category, and any attached **photos and voice memos**. Milestones you do **not**
    share never leave your device.
- **Who can see shared milestones:** only members of a sharing "circle" you created or joined.
  Access is enforced server-side by row-level security; no one else — including other
  Backstory users — can read them.
- **What we do NOT do:** we do not sell or rent this data, do not use it for advertising, do
  not profile you, and do not use it to train AI.
- **Hosting (processor):** account and shared-milestone data is stored with **Supabase**, our
  database/hosting provider, acting on our instructions. It is transmitted over encrypted
  (HTTPS/TLS) connections.

### Information we process

- **The memories you create** — stored on-device only, never transmitted to us, unless you
  explicitly share a specific milestone through Family Sharing (see above).
- **If you create an account:** your email/Apple relay address, an account identifier, your
  chosen display name, and the milestones you choose to share.
- We process **no** usage analytics, advertising data, or location tracking.

### How long we keep it (retention)

On-device data stays on your device **until you delete it or remove the app** — there is no
server-side copy. Deleting a memory removes it (and its photos/voice files) from the device;
deleting the app removes everything on the device.

**If you use an account:** shared milestones stay on our hosting until you unshare them, leave
the circle, or delete your account. **Deleting your account** (Settings → Family sharing →
Delete account) permanently removes your account, your shared milestones, and their uploaded
photos/voice memos from our hosting. Your on-device memories are unaffected.

### Your choices and rights

Because your memories live on your device and under your control, you can exercise data rights
yourself at any time:

- **Access / export (portability):** Settings → _Your memories → Export a backup_ produces a
  complete copy of your data.
- **Erasure:** delete an individual memory from its detail screen, or remove the app to erase
  everything on the device.
- **Account data:** if you created an account, **Delete account** in Settings removes all of
  your server-side data (account, shared milestones, uploaded media). You can also stop sharing
  any milestone or leave a circle at any time.

If you are in a region with privacy laws such as the EU/UK **GDPR** or **California CCPA/CPRA**,
these on-device controls satisfy your access, portability, and deletion rights. Questions:
gauravchoukse22@gmail.com.

### Permissions we ask for (all used only on your device)

- **Photos / Camera** — to attach photos to a milestone.
- **Microphone** — to record a voice memo for a milestone.
- **Calendar** — to find your existing yearly events so you can import them as milestones.
- **Notifications** — to remind you on a milestone's date (scheduled locally on your device).
- **Face ID / biometrics** — to lock the app, if you turn that on.

### Children's privacy

Backstory is intended for **adults** and is **not directed to children** under 13 (or under 16
in the EEA). It does not collect data from anyone, including children.

### A note on sensitive memories

Backstory is often used to hold tender memories, including of people we've lost. Those memories
**never leave your device**, are never analyzed, and are never used for AI or any other purpose.
If they matter to you, we suggest making a **password-protected backup** and keeping the
password somewhere safe (for example, sharing it with someone you trust) so the memories
aren't lost if you lose the device.

### Changes
